## Subheadline Purpose

### What is a Subheadline?
- Comes immediately after hero headline
- Explains the specific benefit
- Answers the "so what?" question
- Sets expectation for what happens when user clicks CTA

### Subheadline Formula
```
[User action] → [Tool does X] → [User gets Y] → [Why it matters]
```

### Example
**Headline**: "Diagnose Incidents in Seconds. Not Hours."
**Subheadline**: "Paste your incident. Get evidence-backed root cause with a fix plan."

This tells user:
1. What to do: Paste incident
2. What tool does: Analyzes it
3. What they get: Root cause + fix plan
4. Why it matters: Based on evidence, not guessing

## Subheadline Characteristics

### What Makes a Good Subheadline
✅ Clear input (what user provides)
✅ Clear output (what user gets)
✅ Benefit articulated (why it matters)
✅ No jargon (accessible to all)
✅ 12-16 words (readable, not too long)
✅ Reinforces headline (consistent message)
✅ Actionable (what user does next)

### What Makes a Bad Subheadline
❌ Vague ("better results")
❌ Too jargony ("evidence-first orchestration")
❌ Too long (>20 words)
❌ Contradicts headline
❌ Doesn't explain what user gets
❌ Marketing fluff with no substance
❌ Unclear benefit
