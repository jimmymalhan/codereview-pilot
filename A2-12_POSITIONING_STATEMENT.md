## Brand Positioning Pillars

### Pillar 1: Specialization (Not Generalist)
**Message**: We're experts in incident diagnosis, not a general-purpose tool.

**Why it matters**: Specialized tools outperform generalists (SRE dashboards are better than Excel spreadsheets).

**Evidence**: Built specifically for SRE use case, 4-stage pipeline optimized for incident diagnosis.

---

### Pillar 2: Evidence-Driven (Not Guessing)
**Message**: Every diagnosis is backed by specific proof.

**Why it matters**: SREs make critical decisions on diagnosis. Proof eliminates escalation anxiety.

**Evidence**: Verifier agent, Skeptic agent, confidence scoring, evidence citations.

---

### Pillar 3: Action-Oriented (Not Analysis)
**Message**: We don't just identify problems. We tell you exactly what to do.

**Why it matters**: SREs need fix plans, not problem summaries.

**Evidence**: Fix recommendations, rollback plans, test requirements.

---

### Pillar 4: Automation (Not Labor)
**Message**: We automate the expensive manual work.

**Why it matters**: RCA automation saves 2+ hours per incident.

**Evidence**: Auto-generated RCA, structured output format, ready to share in 30 seconds.

---

### Pillar 5: Trustworthy (Not Experimental)
**Message**: Built for production, proven by customers, backed by metrics.

**Why it matters**: SREs need reliability, not experiments.

**Evidence**: Customer case studies, confidence scoring, proven methodology.

## Tone of Voice

### How We Speak
- **Clarity over jargon**: Say "root cause" not "causal path analysis"
- **Action-focused**: "Fix this in 2 minutes" not "consider doing this"
- **Evidence-based**: "We measured" not "probably works"
- **Empathetic**: "We know incident response is exhausting" not "haha, you're doing it wrong"
- **Direct**: "This saves you $300K" not "provides significant value"

### What We Avoid
- ❌ Marketing fluff ("Revolutionary", "game-changing")
- ❌ Jargon ("orchestration", "pipeline", "verifier")
- ❌ False claims ("best tool" without proof)
- ❌ Condescension (SREs are experts; we're helpers)
- ❌ Overpromising (we say "reduce" not "eliminate" when appropriate)
