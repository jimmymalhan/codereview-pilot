# Best Sales Guide in the World

**Purpose**: Highest-quality sales practices. Discovery, qualification, value delivery, objection handling, closing. Enterprise and SMB. B2B and product-led. Use when selling, writing sales content, crafting pitches, or training.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Value before pitch** — Understand the buyer's world before presenting. No solution before problem clarity.
2. **Discovery is selling** — Great questions create conviction. Let them say it; don't tell them.
3. **Objections are buying signals** — They signal interest. Address with evidence, not pressure.
4. **Close early and often** — Micro-commitments throughout. "Does that make sense?" "Would you use that?"
5. **Commitment = next step** — Define the next action. No meeting without a clear next step.

---

## Discovery & Qualification

### BANT + Pain + Timeline
- **Budget**: Who holds it? What's the process?
- **Authority**: Who decides? Who influences?
- **Need**: Concrete pain, impact, and cost of inaction
- **Timeline**: Why now? What happens if they wait?

### Discovery Questions (Best-in-Class)
- "What would success look like 90 days after we solve this?"
- "What have you already tried? What worked? What didn't?"
- "Who else is involved in this decision? How do they see it?"
- "If we do nothing, what's the cost in 6 months?"
- "What would need to be true for you to move forward?"

### Qualification Gate
- **Qualified**: Pain + budget + authority + timeline. Proceed.
- **Not qualified**: Educate, nurture, or disqualify fast. No pipeline stuffing.

## Value Proposition

### Structure
1. **Problem** — State their pain in their words (from discovery)
2. **Solution** — One sentence. What you offer.
3. **Differentiation** — Why you, not alternatives
4. **Proof** — Customer result, metric, case study
5. **Ask** — Specific next step

### Formula
> "For [customer type] who [pain], [product] is a [category] that [benefit]. Unlike [alternative], we [differentiation]. [Proof]. [Ask]."

## Objection Handling

### Pattern: Feel–Felt–Find + Evidence
1. **Acknowledge**: "I understand that's a concern."
2. **Validate**: "Others have felt the same."
3. **Reframe**: "What we've found is [evidence, proof]."
4. **Qualify**: "Does that address your concern?"

### Common Objections
| Objection | Response pattern |
|-----------|------------------|
| "Too expensive" | Anchor to cost of inaction. "What's the cost of not solving this?" ROI, payback period. |
| "We need to think about it" | "What specifically would you like to think about?" Uncover real objection. |
| "Send me info" | "Sure. To make it relevant—what's most important: price, timeline, or fit?" Then send tailored info. |
| "We're happy with current vendor" | "What would need to change for you to consider alternatives?" Create opening. |
| "Not the right time" | "What would make it the right time?" Timeline, trigger events. |
| "Need to talk to my team" | "Who? What do they care about? I can help you prepare." |

### Rule: Never argue. Uncover, address with evidence, re-close.

## Closing

### Assumptive Close
- "When would you want to get started—this month or next?"
- "Do you want to start with the standard setup or the expanded one?"

### Summary Close
- Recap agreed value, outcomes, next steps.
- "Based on what we've discussed, the next step is X. Can we schedule that for [date]?"

### Urgency (Ethical Only)
- "Our onboarding slots for [month] fill up by [date]."
- "The annual discount expires [date]. After that, it's list price."

### Micro-Commitments
- "Does that make sense?"
- "Would that solve [pain]?"
- "If we could do that, would you move forward?"

## Enterprise & B2B

### Multi-Stakeholder
- **Champion**: Internal advocate. Arm them with slides, ROI, objections.
- **Economic buyer**: Holds budget. Speak business outcomes, risk, ROI.
- **Technical buyer**: Evaluates fit. Demo, POC, security, integration.
- **Coach**: Tells you the landscape. Use for navigation.

### Sales Cycle
1. **Discovery** — Qualify, map stakeholders, understand process
2. **Value alignment** — Custom value prop per stakeholder
3. **Proof** — Demo, POC, reference call, case study
4. **Proposal** — Tailored to process; include rollback, SLAs
5. **Negotiation** — Concede on non-critical; hold on must-haves
6. **Close** — Signed. Onboarding scheduled. Success criteria defined.

### Deal Registers
- **Green**: On track. Champion confirmed. Next step clear.
- **Yellow**: Risk. Missing info, stalled, or new competitor.
- **Red**: Stuck. Escalate, reframe, or disqualify.

- Proof over claims
